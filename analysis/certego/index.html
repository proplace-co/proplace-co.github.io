Market DEFINITION

Commercial and public-sector real estate managers hire managed physical security providers to deploy electronic access control, video monitoring, and integrated safety systems to guarantee complete property protection. The current market is severely fragmented, leaving property owners to deal with a confusing array of legacy offline locks, broken hardware, and security risks. Upstream sit global hardware suppliers like ASSA ABLOY, while downstream sit local branch technicians, positioning integrated software operators to capture major margins by acting as central platform orchestrators.

Our Market THESIS

The shift from legacy offline mechanical locks to unified, cloud-connected access networks in commercial properties is an irreversible upgrade cycle. Legacy hardware manufacturers cannot easily offering digital-first security services without risking their core wholesale distribution partnerships, which represents their primary financial foundation.

Agile, service-oriented platforms exploit this gap by using localized installation footprints as low-cost customer acquisition hooks to establish high-margin cloud hosting environments. The window of opportunity is wide open as smart-building digital initiatives and security standards expand, closing once regional implementation density reaches market saturation.

Our CONVICTION & WAGER on this Market:

HIGH CONVICTION

While high physical labor requirements limit rapid venture-scale software replication, Certego's strategic control of physical-access touchpoints enables it to maintain a direct customer relationship that pure-play software competitors can never bypass.

We wager that integrated security SaaS platforms in the Nordic region will see a 3x recurring revenue multiple premium over traditional installation services as real estate owners consolidate IT/security budgets. During our first call, we will look at the exact growth vector of hosting fees compared to physical equipment sales to evaluate true scale velocity.

Weaknesses

  • Growth depends on serial acquisitions, which exposes the company to integration delays and margin dilution.
  • No public profile or statements are available for the CEO, limiting external clarity on strategic direction.

  • Rising demand for integrated fire, alarm, and surveillance bundles aligns with Certego's full lifecycle service offering.
  • API connectivity enables partnerships with property-management software providers for embedded security modules.
