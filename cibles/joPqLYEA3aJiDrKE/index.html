How we built this shortlist

To build this list, we focused the search tightly on companies whose core activity is the installation, integration, and maintenance of physical security systems for B2B clients — deliberately setting aside generalist facility services and pure guarding businesses. Every company here was assessed against the same set of concrete criteria, which I have laid out below so you can challenge the logic directly.

Nordic physical security consolidation

Finland, Sweden, Norway, and Denmark share a fragmented landscape of owner-operated integrators — the conditions for a disciplined roll-up are present now, before institutional capital arrives in force.

What we look for

  • Companies whose primary revenue comes from installing and maintaining alarm, access control, or locking systems for commercial or industrial clients
  • Businesses with a meaningful share of recurring maintenance contracts, not just project-based installation revenue
  • Owner-operated or founder-led SMEs with revenue broadly between €2M and €40M, where a conversation can happen without an investment bank in the room
  • Operations concentrated in one or two Nordic countries, giving a clear geographic rationale for combination

What we set aside

  • We set aside companies where physical security is a secondary activity bundled into broader facility management or cleaning contracts — the integration complexity outweighs the benefit
  • We exclude businesses whose revenue is predominantly residential or consumer-facing, as the contract structure and margin profile are fundamentally different from B2B multi-site work
Technical security expansion into DACH

Germany, Austria, and Switzerland have a large installed base of aging security systems and a strong regulatory push toward modernisation — creating a durable demand cycle for specialist integrators.

What we look for

  • Installers and integrators of alarm, access control, or video surveillance systems serving commercial clients in Germany, Austria, or the German- and French-speaking cantons of Switzerland
  • Companies with certified technicians and documented compliance with local security installation standards — a barrier to entry that protects the customer base
  • Businesses with a regional rather than purely local footprint, suggesting they have already demonstrated the ability to manage operations across multiple sites

What we set aside

  • We set aside pure distributors or equipment resellers with no service or maintenance revenue — they add product volume but not the recurring customer relationships that matter here
  • We exclude companies whose growth has been driven primarily by one large public-sector contract, as the revenue concentration creates a risk that does not fit a consolidation logic
Recurring maintenance and remote monitoring

Maintenance contracts and remote monitoring create a revenue base that is largely independent of new construction cycles — this is the financial characteristic that makes a security platform genuinely resilient.

What we look for

  • Operators whose business model includes multi-year maintenance agreements covering alarm systems, access control, or fire detection across multiple client sites
  • Companies offering remote monitoring or alarm response services, which generate monthly recurring revenue and deepen the client relationship
  • Businesses serving clients in sectors with non-negotiable compliance requirements — healthcare, logistics, retail chains — where contract renewal is driven by regulation as much as by satisfaction

What we set aside

  • We set aside businesses where maintenance is offered informally or on a call-out basis only — without structured contracts, the revenue is not predictable enough to anchor a platform valuation
  • We exclude monitoring-only operators with no field installation or maintenance capability, as they cannot serve the full client need and are therefore more vulnerable to displacement
Electromechanical access control and locking specialists

The market-wide shift from mechanical to electromechanical and connected locking systems is generating a sustained replacement cycle — companies already trusted by building owners for this transition hold a structurally advantaged position.

What we look for

  • Companies with demonstrated expertise in both mechanical and electronic locking, able to serve clients at any point in the upgrade journey
  • Businesses that combine hardware installation with software-based access management, creating a stickier relationship than hardware alone
  • Operators serving property managers, logistics operators, or healthcare facilities — sectors where access control is operationally critical and switching costs are high

What we set aside

  • We set aside companies focused exclusively on consumer or residential locking, where the sales model, margin structure, and customer relationship are not compatible with a B2B platform
  • We exclude businesses that act solely as sub-contractors to larger integrators, as they have no direct client relationship to bring into the platform
